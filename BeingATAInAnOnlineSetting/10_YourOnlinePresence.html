Constructing presence in the online learning environment is important to establishing a successful online community of learners.

How instructors and learners see each other as “real people” in the online environment  is important to online learners as it combats feelings of isolation and gives learners a feeling of community.

Interactivity in online courses, particularly between the student and TA, can play an important role both in student satisfaction and persistence.

Your presence sets the tone for the whole class. While you do not need to be available all the time, provide a schedule of when you and your teaching team will be available for questions, and include when you will be monitoring asynchronous communication.

 

It's As Easy As

 

  • If you share your UToronto email with your students to contact you, set aside time to respond to them
  • Provide guidelines for communicating with your students in an announcement or during the first day of class
  • For example: I will respond to emails within 2 days, if I do not respond by then, please reach out to me again
  • Use the word ‘drop-in’ hours instead of office hours
  • Something as little as changing the wording can make the environment more comfortable for students.
  • e.g. Weekly Drop In-Hours: hangout and get some help!
  • Online drop -in hours allows students to drop in and ask questions; it is an informal opportunity to chat making it attractive for everyone involved. 
  • Offering a connection point that allows students a chance to meet with you directly, even for a short period of time, is a relationship and confidence builder.
