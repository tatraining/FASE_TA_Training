Using Active Learning in BB Collaborate:
  • Start by creating shared norms for participation and setting the stage and tone of the class discussion
  • Allow students to suggest/contribute to the ground rules for online discussions
  • Provide prompts or goals that encourage discussion to guide learning.
  • Provide a problem to solve related to the topic of the course. Have students discuss in small breakout groups, and check-in occasionally.

Why Use Breakout Groups?
  • Using breakout groups is especially important for first years. It provides a peer or student group for new students who don’t know anyone yet. Breakout groups can help them become part of a community.
  • Using breakout groups may make your tutorial more intriguing to students; they will attend tutorials to meet people.
  • By creating smaller groups, students are able to connect and meet people outside their demographic.
  • These smaller groups also help students become more comfortable speaking in a group.
  • It is also important to reshuffle groups so they can meet new people, and improve communication and collaboration skills.
