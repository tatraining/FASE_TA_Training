Sending frequent announcements is a great way to show students that you are there to help them and care about their success. 

If you have not used Announcements before, watch the video on How to Send Announcements in Quercus

You Can Use Announcements to:

1. Send a welcome video or introduction paragraph before your first tutorial.
This allows students to get to know more about you on a personal level.

For Example:

 

2. Check-in with your students.
Put timed announcement for release for Monday, Wednesday, and Friday to help guide students to where they should be in the course at that point. Even something as little as weekly or monthly check-ins to remind students about upcoming assignments, etc. shows that you care about their success.    

For Example:

 

 

3. Answer questions from tutorials that were unable to be answered.

Whether you ran out of time or didn't know the answer, follow-up with that student. A critical sign that you care is responding to student questions. However, no one knows all the answers. You can ask the course prof, a fellow TA, use the textbook, and the web. By doing so you are modelling the professional problem solving that every student needs to be successful no matter where they end up.  Do not feel embarrassed or incompetent, no one makes it alone in life these days, so, these interpersonal skills and this community mentality to problem-solving is also crucial for future success!
