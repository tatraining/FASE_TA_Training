You may have a few questions about making this "personal connection" with students ...

 

How did TAs make this connection during in-person sessions?

Anecdotal evidence suggests that during in-person sessions, UofT engineering students were more likely to trust TAs that inquired about their personal wellbeing. Students also mentioned that they preferred TAs who were enthusiastic about course material and were willing to “dumb down” their explanations.
 

How personal should this be?

You need become a mentor that your students can trust their academic weaknesses with. However, don’t aim to become best friends with any student, this would create a conflict of interest. Moderation is key.
 

How can I make the right kind of connection?

Show the students that you genuinely care about their academic wellbeing in the course. As a pre-requisite, student success and providing quality instruction need to be legitimate concerns to you.
