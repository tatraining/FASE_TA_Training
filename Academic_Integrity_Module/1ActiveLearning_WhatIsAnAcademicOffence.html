The following examples are academic offences committed by students

 

"A student in MAT186 (Calculus 1) brings a Canon F-570SG scientific calculator when the course only allows Casio FX-991 od Sharp EL-520 scientific calculators."

"A student asks his friend to complete his TopHat quiz."

"A student modifies his elder sister's old Driving Event assignment from ESP I and submits it as his"

"A Praxis I team copy-pastes and edits a Wikipedia article for an assignment. They then cite the Wikipedia sources rather than the actual Wikipedia page."

"A student, upon recieving her MAT188 (Linear Algebra) midterm test, erases and corrects a potion of a written answer. She then submits the test for reevaluation."

"An ESP II team buys a .org domain and publishes false claims on it that support their design decision. They then use it as a citation for an appendix."

"A student uses the the Inspect Element feature to temporarily alter how their grades appear on Acorn. They use a screenshot of these grades to apply for a summer lab-assistant position."

 
 
 

The following examples are academic offences committed by faculty members/TAs

 

"A TA allows a student to use an unauthorized calculator during a tutorial quiz because no one has an extra authorized calculator"

"A Department Chair accepts a student into their program even though there are more qualified candidates because the student is the Chair's child"

"A TA gives a passing grade to an ESP group's dismal Conceptual Design Specification because one of the students is dating the TA's cousin"

"An instructor boasts about how innovative a student was in their thesis course so that the instructor's colleague may accept the student into their graduate program"
