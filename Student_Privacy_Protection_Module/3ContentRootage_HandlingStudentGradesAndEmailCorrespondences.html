At the start of the semester’s tutorials/practicals, you should inform the students on how you will be posting/disclosing their grades. If given the opportunity, which option should you choose?

Verbally tell students their grades

Do Not Do
Email grades to the students

Avoid If Possible
Post grades on Quercus

Best Practice
Print the grades on a paper and display it in the tutorial/practical room/lab

Avoid If Possible


Remember that confidentiality is an objective when posting grades. Try to make sure that only the student knows their corresponding grade.

 
 

When compelled to email grades to students, which practice do you think is best?

Use your UofT email to send a list of grades to all your student

Do Not Do
Use your UofT email to send the corresponding grade to each student’s UofT email

Best Practice
Use any other email other than your UofT email to send grades

Do Not Do
Send grades to any other email other than an UofT email

Do Not Do

  • Inform students you are expected to only correspond from your UofT email with their UofT emails
  • Do not correspond with non-UofT emails
  • Write emails in a professional manner and scope (e.g. no slang, no discussions on personal matters, etc.)
  • Avoid using the “reply all” feature in email threads to keep recipient identities confidential from the whole group
  • Use the “bcc” (blind carbon copy) feature when practical
  • Do not share student emails or email addresses unless needed
  • Do not forward student emails to individuals not involved in the course

When compelled to email grades to students, which practice do you think is best?

Display the students work after informing the student that you are going to do so

Do Not Do
Ask the student’s legal guardian for consent to display the student’s work

Do Not Do
Use the assignment without asking for the student’s consent

Do Not Do
Ask the student for written consent to display their work

Best Practice


You can only reveal a student’s grade or work to a third party (e.g. family, guardians, friends, colleagues, etc.) if you have the student’s FULL WRITTEN CONSENT.

When compelled to email grades to students, what should you use to organize the grades?

Last 4 digits of the student number

Best Practice
First name as well as the last 4 digits of the student number

Do Not Do
Full name

Do Not Do
Full student number

Do Not Do


A publicly viewable document (in this context) refers to a document that someone other than you or the student the grade addresses can view. When compelled to post grades on such documents never associate the grades with full names or complete student numbers.
