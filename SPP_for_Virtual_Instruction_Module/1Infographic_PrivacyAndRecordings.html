When instructing in a virtual environment, advance notice must be given to students as to how their information will be used.

 

When informing students, remember the What-Why-Who procedure.
Tell the students:

What information is being collected
 
e.g. chats, screen recordings, audio recordings, etc.
Students must know, prior to the date of the tutorial/practical in question, what forms of information will be recorded/archived
 
 
Why information is being collected
 
e.g. To allow students in different time-zones to view the tutorial
The prinicple purpose for the recording/archiving student data must be disclosed to the student prior to the data being collected
 
 
Who they can contact regarding the information collected
 
e.g. department administrator, course coordinator, etc.
Make sure you discuss with your course coordinator whom to reccomend to students if they want further clarification regarding the data collection
 

Keep in mind, all of this applies to both virtual students and in-person students (e.g. recordings of in-person sessions). Also note that if the session is not recorded/archived in any way, no notice needs to be provided as this is akin to a non-recorded in-person session.

 
 

Example scenario:
You are a TA for MIE100: Dynamics. When you meet with your course instructor, they inform you that only tutorials for Section 0101 will be recorded. You ask the professor who you can refer students to if they have questions about the recordings. She tells you that these students should be referred to the course coordinator. You also ask your professor if you should make announcements about the recordings and she gives you the go-ahead. Later that day you and your fellow TAs send out an announcement about all TUT0101 sessions being recorded. It says that these recordings are to allow students to access tutorial discussions at any time and that if students have any concerns regarding the recordings, they should consult the course coordinator.
